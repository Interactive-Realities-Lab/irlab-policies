# IRLab Graduate Student Research Requirements

This document specifies the IRLab research expectations for students pursuing the MS and PhD degrees.

## MS Requirements

All MS students in IRLab are required to complete a **thesis**.

The resulting thesis work must be of sufficient quality and maturity to be **suitable for publication at a high-impact journal or conference**, such as:

* IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics (IEEE TVCG)
* IEEE Virtual Reality (IEEE VR)

## PhD Requirements

### 1. Research Proficiency Exam

Prior to the **Research Proficiency Exam**, the student must have:

* completed **at least one original research project**;
* conducted a **user study** as part of that project; and
* written a **first-author paper** suitable for publication at a **high-impact conference or journal**.

This requirement may be fulfilled through a **published paper** or a **paper that is soon to be submitted**, provided that it is based on the student's **MS thesis work completed at Iowa State University**.

### 2. Preliminary Oral Exam

Prior to or at the **Preliminary Oral Exam**, the student must:

* have a total of **at least two first-author papers** published in a **high-impact conference or journal**; and
* present a proposal for the completion of **at least two new research contributions** suitable for publication at a **high-impact venue**.

### 3. Final Oral Exam

Prior to or at the **Final Oral Exam**, the student must:

* have **at least three first-author papers published** in a **high-impact conference or journal**; and
* have **one additional completed research contribution submitted for publication as first author** at a **high-impact venue**.

## Notes

* "High-impact venue" refers to selective, well-recognized journals and conferences in areas relevant to IRLab research.
* Example venues include **IEEE TVCG** and **IEEE VR**, though other comparable venues may also satisfy the requirement.
* All listed publication-related milestones are expected to reflect the student's **primary intellectual leadership**, as indicated by **first authorship**.
